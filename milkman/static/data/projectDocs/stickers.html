Tools used:

  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Sketching

Result (TL;DR):

I created the following set of stickers based on classes in the UMich School of Information. The goal was to develop a consistent style that could be used across numerous specific instances, while exercising my ability to develop original semiotics.

Process

  1. Sketching

    I started with lots of sketches to flesh out my ideas. Particularly when trying to develop easily recognizable icons and semiotics, I found this was particularly helpful, as it got all of the boring and basic ideas out of the way. Here are some of them; I'm not going to show all of the gibberish, as most of it really doesn't pertain.

  2. Basic Boi

    At this point, I was still uninspired, but created the following stickers. I'm including them now not because I think they have value in their final result, but because they showed me what I wanted to avoid. While I like the idea of visualizing the transformation of the student through taking the class, that's about all I like here. These stickers lacked unity, creativity, and any sort of meaningful presence, which directed my next iteration.

  3. Starting Over

    I wanted to create something that people would actually want to put on a laptop or notebook. In a school of designers, this meant it had to have modern, striking visual appeal. At the time, I was also into playing with figure/ground dynamics. All changes and ideas considered, my result is below. I'd be much happier putting these on my notebook.

    Honestly, I think these designs may translate even better to a series of posters...
